# HELLDECK Taste Brief

## Core Belief
The best party games don't write jokes FOR you — they create the conditions where YOUR group becomes the joke. HELLDECK believes AI should be invisible infrastructure that serves chaos, not a content author seeking credit. Every card should feel like it came from inside the room.

## Identity Anchors

### What HELLDECK believes that competitors don't:
- One phone, passed around, is more intimate than everyone on their own screen
- AI-generated content should be indistinguishable from human-curated content — if you can tell it's AI, it failed
- "Appropriate" is the death of funny. The spice slider exists so groups can find THEIR line, not ours
- Learning what makes YOUR group laugh is more valuable than having 10,000 generic cards
- Offline-first isn't a compromise — it's a feature. No WiFi excuses. No server outages. Just play.

### Three products in this space that are painfully generic:
1. **Cards Against Humanity clones** — Same fill-in-the-blank mechanic, same shock-value-as-substitute-for-wit formula, same "edgy = funny" assumption. They confuse offensive with creative.
2. **Corporate party game apps** — Sanitized, focus-grouped, afraid of their own audience. Every card reads like it was approved by legal. The humor is beige.
3. **Trivia games pretending to be party games** — Knowledge-testing dressed up as social play. No vulnerability, no personal stakes, no reason to laugh at your friend specifically.

### Three non-competitor references that capture the right feeling:
1. **"Uncut Gems" (2019)** — Relentless tension, can't look away, everyone in the room is reacting. The pacing never lets you breathe.
2. **A late-night diner at 3am** — Fluorescent lighting, cheap coffee, conversations that get too real. Ugly-beautiful.
3. **Tyler, the Creator's album art direction** — Bold colors on black, aggressive typography, refuses to be tasteful in the conventional sense but has unmistakable taste.

### After 30 seconds with HELLDECK, someone should feel:
Adrenaline. The specific feeling of "oh no, this is going to get personal" mixed with "I can't wait." Not comfort — anticipation. The same energy as when someone says "okay but don't tell anyone this" in a group chat.

### The one thing HELLDECK will absolutely NEVER do:
Apologize for being too much. No "family-friendly mode" as the default. No watered-down prompts to avoid offending. The spice slider goes DOWN if you want it to — the baseline is chaos.

## Visual Taste Principles
- **Neon on void**: High-energy accent colors (#FF2768 magenta-red, #CBFF4D radioactive lime, #4DF2FF cyan) on near-black (#060608). This isn't "dark mode" — it's a room with the lights off and the phone screen blazing.
- **Party-readable**: 34sp display, 60dp touch targets. Designed for the person three seats over and the person who's had four drinks. Legibility is non-negotiable.
- **Haptic punctuation**: Vibration isn't decoration — it's rhythm. Phase changes hit. Vote locks thud. The phone is a percussion instrument in the game's flow.
- **Zero polish**: Not rough because we couldn't make it smooth — rough because smooth is boring. Smooth is a banking app. HELLDECK is a mosh pit with rules.

## Copy Taste Principles
- **Second person, present tense, imperative mood**: "You must reply." "Vote now." "Pick your victim." Commands, not suggestions.
- **Slang as native language**: "Cap," "banger," "trash" aren't borrowed — they're the vocabulary. If a word feels like it has air quotes around it, cut it.
- **Brevity as aggression**: Short sentences hit harder. The game doesn't explain itself twice. You either get it or you're about to learn.
- **The swap test**: If you can replace "HELLDECK" with "Party Game App" and nothing breaks, the copy is dead. Every line should be unmistakably this game.

## Code Taste Principles
- **Domain language over framework language**: `SpiceSlider` not `RangeInput`. `HelldeckEngine` not `GameManager`. Names should smell like the game.
- **Content system sophistication**: Three-tier fallback (AI -> Gold -> Template) with Thompson Sampling isn't over-engineering — it's the core product. The content system IS the taste.
- **Party-proof architecture**: Every interaction designed for imprecision. Fat touch targets, forgiving timing, graceful state recovery. The code assumes chaos.
