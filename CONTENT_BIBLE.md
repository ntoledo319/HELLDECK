# CONTENT_BIBLE.md — The writing law for HELLDECK content

_Binding for every card written for HELLDECK 2.0 (The Descent — HDRealRules2.md). Derived from the full-corpus autopsy (CARD_AUTOPSY.md), Jackbox/CAH craft research, humor theory, and 9 simulated playtests. A card that breaks a law does not ship, no matter how funny it is._

## Voice

A dive bar at 2am. A group chat that went too far. Affectionate violence. The card is a friend proposing something horrible — never a listicle headline, never a corporate icebreaker, never a tweet. UNHINGED, never WHOLESOME. Every card must read aloud in one breath.

## The two-axis rating (replaces "spice")

Every card carries **EXPOSURE (E)** and **CHAOS (C)**, each 1–5:
- **E = how personal.** What answering/being-targeted truthfully confesses about a present person. E is what consent ceilings cap. E5 = real romantic/sexual/shame history behind a deniability frame.
- **C = how deranged.** Feral imagery, dark premises, absurdity. **C is never capped and never low.** C≥3 at EVERY exposure tier — "feral-but-impersonal" (E1/C5: shovel-bleach-tarp, crimes against mannequins) is the backbone of low-E play. A card that could open a corporate offsite (E≤2, C≤2) does not exist in this game.
- Dark premises need a benign frame (fictional distance, "would," euphemism). Dark WITHOUT a frame is an interrogation, not a joke (the body-count class) — kill.

## THE TEN COMMANDMENTS

1. **Film a scene, never deliver a verdict.** Concrete act + place/object/number/time ("40 cans of beans," "2am," "the trunk"). Banned words in card text: unhinged, chaotic, toxic, main character, NPC, "gives off X energy," "most likely to be [adjective]." The card shows; the table judges.
2. **The card asks; the room answers.** If the joke is complete on the card, players are a laugh track and the deck decays like CAH. No prewritten punchlines, no twist endings, no trailing tags ("They knew."). Setups end where the story begins.
3. **Pass the contest test, both directions.** Roast/vote cards: name the 2–3 friend archetypes who'd split the vote — universal behaviors (everyone qualifies) and single-archetype behaviors (one obvious victim) both fail. Answer cards: 3 different funny answers in 10 seconds or kill.
4. **E prices exposure; C is never the price.** No icebreakers at any tier. No "edgy vocabulary = high spice" (EDGING-the-taboo-word is E1). Re-rate everything; trust nothing from the old corpus.
5. **The third-party firewall.** Above E3: no card whose truthful answer names or implicates specific present people (the "in this room" desire/history class is dead), none whose answer can out orientation, fidelity, or health. Ever: no appearance, no family death, no protected traits.
6. **Zero shelf-life vocabulary.** No meme nouns or brands as punchlines (OnlyFans, LinkedIn, feet pics, mugshots, situationship, Spotify Wrapped, Funko, NFTs, therapy-speak…). Brands only as neutral setting (a Costco can host a story; "crying at Costco" can't be the joke). If it quotes 2021 Twitter, it dies with 2021 Twitter. If the debate existed on Reddit before 2020 (duck-sized horses, pineapple pizza), it does not exist here.
7. **Skeleton budget: max 2 cards per skeleton per deck, and no joke exists twice anywhere in the corpus.** Run dedup on verb+shape within decks and across decks before anything goes gold (the old corpus shipped 15 cross-deck duplicate jokes).
8. **Build the table-aware lane.** Each deck carries its quota of cards referencing the present room ("blackmail material on everyone in this room," {NAME} slots) — the only structurally un-copyable content. {NAME} never appears in a slot that implicates them factually above E3 (firewall applies).
9. **Written to be spoken.** One breath to read, <2 seconds to glance the reveal, performance-first where the mechanic performs (if a card is equally funny read silently, it's a tweet — cut it). Prefer cards a performer can act out; seed inherently funny concrete words (eulogy, silverback, thirst trap).
10. **Register quotas (the anti-monoculture law).** Per deck: ≤35% relatable-observational (the old corpus was 84%); the rest spread across absurdist/escalation, deadpan understatement, sustained menace, petty-domestic, gross-out/body, physical, and high-register parody (legalese, nature-doc, noir, corporate-memo). Reference worlds beyond phones/dating/therapy are mandatory: food, animals, history/myth, small-town, trades, sports, medicine, nature. ≤20% of any deck may reference a phone/app at all (old corpus: 38%).

## House moves (encouraged)

- **The euphemism engine**: self-incriminating reframes that open loops ("...and call it research," "celebrating life") — hands the table a defense to demolish.
- **Two-axis vote tension**: crime × incompetence ("Who would accidentally send a voice note of themselves talking trash?") — different players vote on different logic; the argument is the game.
- **Benign violation done right**: genuinely dark premise + fictional distance + flattering-damning trait ("Who would befriend their kidnapper within the first hour?").
- **One anchor + two open slots** (story games): exactly one interrogable concrete detail, at least two blanks the performer must fill live.
- **Accidental-confession framing** (speed games): second-person categories where desperate blurts read as self-exposure with speed as deniability.

## Per-deck laws (summary — full requirements in the autopsy JSONs)

**Roast Consensus (150+):** two-victim test; scene-not-verdict; ~30% past-tense story-mining ("Who HAS…", E≤3); 20+ table-aware cards; euphemism engine as house style.
**Fill-In Finisher (150+):** ≥10 distinct structures (old deck: 41% two skeletons); ≥40% performance-first (comedy exists only ALOUD — speech acts: eulogies, voicemails, last words); write-verb only (invented punchlines, never true-history recall); ≥25% {NAME}/room hooks (old: zero); every setup seeds one concrete comic element.
**Over/Under (150+):** ≥70% phone-receipt-resolvable in <10s (name the receipt surface on the card); counts timeboxed (today/this week/this month — never lifetime); non-receipt counts pride-valenced (high number = legend, never shame-tally); line-debatability test (conflicting priors about THIS person or kill).
**Confession or Cap (150+):** plausibility-window test (must be arguable both believe AND cap); one anchor + two open slots; POV-universal and uncheckable (lockable TRUE by anyone at the table); logic must survive 5s of jury scrutiny; setup-never-punchline.
**Scatterblast (80):** category+letter only (the bomb owns timing); letter audit (10 valid answers aloud or change the letter); ≥50% second-person accidental-confession categories; desperation-arc test (the 8th answer must be funnier than the 1st); ≤25% "Things…" openers; ≤7 words, shoutable.
**Poison Pitch (100):** balance gate (a strong pitch for EACH side in 30s; blind poll ≤65/35); contrast rule (poisons of different flavors — body vs reputation, private vs public); angle density ≥3 per option; performability bonus (options a pitcher can act out).
**Red Flag Rally (100):** one card = one joke = one source of truth (display and data fields must match — old deck: 19/50 mismatched); perk and flag on orthogonal axes (perk genuinely tempting to a stranger, flag independently disqualifying — no perk-negation reveals); split-room test (write the defender's argument AND the room's counter-heckle; either silent = dead); ≥4 rotating structures; state evidence flat, never editorialize.
**Alibi Drop (80):** words are contraband, not answers (orthogonal to the accusation; if a word could be the actual explanation, it's dead); lineup-compatible (1–2 word common nouns that sit beside 5 decoys); accusations ≤14 words, absurd-but-answerable, with one forensic detail; no word repeats deck-wide; ~20% room-history accusations; each card ships WITH its 5 decoy words.
**Text Thread Trap (60 + tones):** left-on-read test (the text must demand a reply); punchline amputation (the sender never jokes — the performer is the comedian); open-blank rule (the incriminating thing is never specified); ≤4 cards per sender archetype; 8–10 performance set-pieces (the reply is a bit); {NAME} lane added (old: zero).
**Reality Check (60):** two-gate test (room holds evidence + subject's ego is invested); ban "how many times" counts (no perception gap); 1–10 only, direction locked; mine Dunning-Kruger domains (driving, humor, temper, being over an ex); seed live-demo dimensions (interrupting, taking a joke — the debate performs the test).
**Taboo Timer (60):** BODY-COUNT-test list-writing (speak the 3 clues a drunk clue-giver gives; every load-bearing word goes on the list); pop-culture route audit (song/movie sharing the name goes on the list or the word dies); aliveness gate (alive in 2026, survives 24 months); ≤20% per topic cluster; forbidden lists cover stems/inflections.
**Hot Seat Imposter (60):** observation test (≥2 people in the room plausibly hold evidence; ban "nobody knows"/"secretly"); resolvable ground truth in <10s (story or on-the-spot receipt); performance over trivia (never one-token answers); design for the LIE (a wrong-but-believable answer should roast the target); ≤40% one skeleton; ≤2 phone-contents cards.
**Title Fight (40):** every card names what fighters DO and what the crowd votes on; ≥1/3 physical/performative duels (straight-face duel, slow-mo reenactment, hype-man-off); both corners defensible in 10s; {NAME}/self-testimony leverage ("cite your worst ___; room votes"); the Reddit-canon ban list is absolute.

## The funnel (no card skips a stage)

1. **Draft** against this bible (register + E/C assigned at write time).
2. **Self-cull**: writer kills bottom third before submission (Jackbox: reject any prompt you can't answer 3 ways fast).
3. **Dedup**: skeleton + verb-shape scan within deck and cross-deck.
4. **Mechanic gate**: per-deck test (two-victim / 3-answers / balance / receipt / plausibility-window / letter audit…).
5. **Ban screens**: vocabulary expiry, third-party firewall, wholesome test ("could this run at an HR offsite?" → kill), specificity test ("does anything break if this card moves to a generic party deck?" → if no, kill).
6. **Table read**: performed aloud in the target register; if it's equally funny silent, cut.
7. **Gold**: E/C rated, decoys/receipts/forbidden-lists attached per deck schema, telemetry-tracked from night one (🔥 rates drive culls in content drops).
