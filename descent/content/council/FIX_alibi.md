# COUNCIL VERDICT — ALIBI
scores: funny 6/10 | mechanic-fit 8/10 | addictiveness 6/10 -> fix-then-ship

## THE DECK IN ONE SENTENCE
A deck with a genuinely vicious, room-pointing accusation spine that a friend group will keep dealing, undercut by word-lineups that go unsmuggle-ably obscure and lock into learnable category templates exactly where the mechanic should tighten.

## FULL SUMMARY
Two decks stapled together. The accusation half is the real thing and the reason to keep dealing: E4/E5 point at real people in the room (ex is your emergency contact/landlord/dentist; the second wallet you switch to when the check hits; the secret group vote on who gets cut; the folder marked 'leverage') — situational, affectionately vicious, DISTINCTIVE, and endlessly replayable because the dread is about the actual table. That layer alone is an 8, which is why mechanic-fit rated the deck highly: the accusations beg to be lied about and confusability is genuinely engineered (same-family lineups, doubled domains) rather than odd-one-out. But ALIBI is a two-sided game and the JURY plays the word-lineup every round, and the lineups are where it sags. Two convergent breaks: (1) the late E4 music block buries unsmuggle-able words (theorbo, sarangi, ophicleide, doumbek) in PLANT slots — the difficulty curve is inverted, going lexically obscure exactly when escalation should be SOCIAL; and (2) four rigid category-triplet templates each run 15-26 cards, so the jury learns to slot-anchor and the deduction goes slack, while same-category decoys collapse the real 3-of-8 read into three 1-of-2 guesses. Softer drags: a generic farm-animal-in-weird-place opening, a funeral/death crutch that becomes a formula by mid-deck, a repeated staccato tic, and E5's best premises paired with its deadest cheese/dance/fabric word sets. None of this touches the accusations — the single highest-leverage pass is to regenerate the word-lineups around sayable, clangy, cross-domain nouns with varied template shapes, push all obscurity into decoy-only slots, and cap/vary the funeral and farm-animal blocks. Do that and it jumps two points and buys a third and fourth night. Fix-then-ship.

## THE FIX LIST (implement ALL of these)
[1] TARGET: alibi_v3_070
    PROBLEM: All-lens agreement: the late E4 music block (063-072) puts near-unknowable words in PLANT slots — theorbo, sarangi, shehnai, ophicleide, doumbek, quena, zurna, sarod, crumhorn, nadaswaram. You cannot smuggle a word you can't pronounce or define into a 30s lie; the smuggle becomes a neon flare, and the jury can't reason about categories it doesn't recognize. 070 is worst (all three plants unknowable), then 065, 072, 067, 064, 069, 063.
    REQUIRED ACTION: Keep at most ONE exotic plant per card; swap the rest to sayable instruments (pompano->tuna, shehnai->flute, theorbo->cello, doumbek->drum, quena->flute, zurna->oboe, sarod->banjo). Push the obscure names into DECOYS — decoys only need to confuse, never be spoken.

[2] TARGET: alibi_v3_029
    PROBLEM: Mechanic + Addiction: four rigid category-triplet templates each run 15-26 cards straight (009-028 instrument+finance+animal; 029-054 herb+profession+tool; 055-075 animal+world-instrument+landform; 076-088 cheese+dance+fabric). The jury learns to slot-anchor the three domains by mid-first-night, so the deduction goes slack and the read barely needs the alibi. This staleness-caps half the table every round — the biggest 'one more round' killer.
    REQUIRED ACTION: Break the fixed formulas: vary semantic categories card-to-card and mix template shapes — some single-category, some tri, some pairs — so the jury can never pattern-match which slot holds which domain.

[3] TARGET: alibi_v3_014
    PROBLEM: Mechanic + Addiction: in the tri-category cards the 5 decoys are same-category as the plants (2 instrument decoys, 2 finance decoys, etc.) plus a dead furniture+food singleton pair (chandelier/marmalade, larder/gnocchi). This degrades 'which 3 of 8 were forced' into three independent 1-of-2 slot guesses, and the unmatched furniture/food slots are dead — no informed jury ever picks them.
    REQUIRED ACTION: Seed cross-category decoys so a plant can't be located by its slot, and spend the two spare decoys as a 3rd instrument / 3rd animal rather than an unmatched furniture+food pair, keeping every lineup slot a live candidate.

[4] TARGET: alibi_v3_082
    PROBLEM: Laugh + Addiction: E5 has the deck's best accusations (leverage folder, life-insurance beneficiary, goodbye-forever notes) saddled with its deadest word sets — homogeneous cheese/dance/fabric/gem taxonomies (provolone/roquefort/camembert) that are boring to shoehorn and reduce the jury to a memory test. The mechanic's biggest laughs come from words that CLANG (walrus, alimony, ferret, ocarina).
    REQUIRED ACTION: Repoint E5 plant/decoy sets toward concrete absurd nouns — animals, weird-but-sayable instruments, bureaucratic finance terms — the way 014 (trombone/mortgage/seagull) does. Keep the killer accusations, swap the words.

[5] TARGET: alibi_v3_001
    PROBLEM: Laugh + Addiction: the E1 on-ramp opens on the deck's weakest, most generic cards — 001/002/004/007 are all farm-animal-in-weird-place (goat/goat/cow/goldfish + object), interchangeable AI-improv random-noun soup that fails the specificity test. First impressions matter and this is one joke told four times.
    REQUIRED ACTION: Give the animal a POV or point it at a player ('the goat's lanyard has YOUR name on it'), and add a petty, human, non-livestock opener so the on-ramp shows range before the great mid/late accusations land.

## CROSS-GAME DIRECTIVES (apply to this deck too)
- FIX THE ARC GRAMMAR SO SPOTLIGHT GAMES REACH DEPTH 5 AND 7 (packages/engine/src/arc.ts). Verified: at depth 5 the forced placements — simultaneous opener at slot 0, scatter at mid(2), bargain at bargainSlotFor(5)=3, all-players-score finale at 4 — consume every slot at idx>=3, and the spotlight gate blocks idx<3. Depth 7 is identical: scatter at 3, titlefight at 5, bargain at 4, finale at 6. Result: CONFESSION, POISON, REDFLAG and ALIBI can only ever be dealt in a Full Damnation.
  WHY: This is the single highest-leverage item in the entire review and no deck-level chair could see it. Confession is the best-scoring deck in the box (8/8/7) and the default 'Standard Descent' never deals it. Most groups' first and possibly only night is drawn from just four decks with sanctioned repeats. Every card fix below is worth less than making the good decks actually reachable. Loosen the spotlight gate to idx>=2, or make the bargain/finale slots spotlight-eligible, or drop the forced titlefight at depth 7 — then re-run the 1000-seed property tests.
- BREAK THE CROSS-DECK ROMANCE MONOCULTURE AT E4/E5. Cap romance/exes/hookups at roughly one third of every deck's deep tiers and backfill with non-romance archetypes (the cheap one, the petty accountant, the snitch, the grudge-holder, the menace, table-aware implication). Roast E4/E5 is almost all dating; confession's entire E5 is sexual; overunder's finale is ~15 flavors of ex-pining; fillin's E5 is sex/shame confessions; redflag's summit is partner-scorecards.
  WHY: Five of nine chairs found this independently INSIDE their own deck without knowing it was universal. Across a full night it compounds catastrophically: every game's biggest moment lands on the same topic, so the same one or two 'romantic' people get named at every climax. That is typecasting, it is the fastest way to make one specific friend stop wanting to play, and the consent ceilings do not catch it because it is a frequency problem, not an exposure-tier problem.
- REBUILD THE MIDDLES (E2/E3), NOT THE PEAKS — this is the 'everyone has fun' fix. Every deck's identified sag block sits in its mid tier: roast 082-096, poison 048-067, scatter's 34-card E3 plateau, overunder's E2 (half the deck), alibi's funeral belly, fillin's court-record family, redflag's roommate band. Thread table-aware 'someone in this room' spikes through these middles rather than banking them at the end.
  WHY: consent.ts caps a mixed room at the second-lowest ceiling (min-of-all at N<=4), which means any group with one cautious friend plays almost entirely in E2/E3 — exactly the material nine councils just condemned. Right now the game's quality is inversely correlated with how mixed the group is. Fixing the middles also fixes 'the night sags before it peaks' for every group, so this single pass serves both questions the owner asked.
- GIVE EVERY DECK A REAL C2/E1 ON-RAMP. Roast's chaos floor never drops below C3, confession's floor is C3, redflag OPENS at full menace (go-bag/vanish, padlocked chest freezer, deadbolt on the outside sitting in E1). Add a small low-stakes-but-still-pointed opening rung to each deck and demote the misplaced menace cards out of E1.
  WHY: Three chairs flagged this separately. The first two circles are when a mixed group decides whether this is their kind of game or something they need to survive. Opening hot is how you lose the two most cautious people at the table in the first ten minutes — and once they disengage, the rest of the night's consensus votes have fewer live candidates and the whole engine degrades.
- GLOBAL TEMPLATE-FATIGUE CULL: cut roughly 10-15% of every deck, specifically its dominant repeated skeleton. Fillin's ~20 'official record euphemizes my crime' cards, redflag's ~25-30 scorecard cards, poison's ~20 annoying-friend block, alibi's four rigid category-triplet templates (each running 15-26 cards straight), scatter's ~50-of-81 first-person confessional shape, overunder's ~25 physical stunts, confession's ~15 self-justification tags. Target ~120-150 cards deleted corpus-wide.
  WHY: 987 cards is the headline number; effective variety is far lower and every chair said so in different words. Template repetition is what makes a table say 'this again' on night two, and it is the specific tell the project's own taste law bans as content-farm output. Elimination before addition is already the house rule — the corpus is big enough to cut hard without falling below coverage.
