# COUNCIL VERDICT — TITLEFIGHT
scores: funny 6/10 | mechanic-fit 6/10 | addictiveness 5/10 -> fix-then-ship

## THE DECK IN ONE SENTENCE
A genuinely fun roast-to-their-face physical spike whose top half is choke-laugh gold, but it barely delivers the "physical" it promises, half the roster reskins one crowd-vote template, and 10 fixed duels go stale the same night they fire twice.

## FULL SUMMARY
Unanimous fix-then-ship. The ceiling is proven and high: tf_010 (meanest impression to each other's face), tf_009 (seduce the table), tf_006 (villain monologue), tf_003 (hype-man walkout) and tf_004 (embarrassing-fall reenactment) are DISTINCTIVE, roast-native, crowd-votable, table-personal — they could only exist in this game. That's the deck's spine; keep it. Three problems drag the score to 5-6. (1) STALENESS: 10 fixed duels is too thin for a spike that fires multiple times a night; effective variety is really ~5-6 because ~3 duds get skipped and the best get over-picked. All three lenses want the pool grown to ~24. (2) TEMPLATE + NO PHYSICALITY (same defect, two framings): the mechanic promises a PHYSICAL palate-cleanser but 8-9 of 10 are talk/perform, and those 8 collapse into one 'perform a bit, crowd votes' loop that goes limp by the fourth draw — the fix is to cap crowd-judged performance at ~half and add genuinely physical, visible, objective duels. (3) NO BELT META: the mechanic hands out a belt every fight and forgets it — add a champion-defends/grudge-rematch layer so rivalries compound across the night. Card-level, four fixes matter most: anchor tf_008's trash talk to a concrete target, reward the most PATHETIC (not 'believable') fake-cry in tf_005 and time-box it, make tf_002's thumb war visible/loud instead of silent-self-adjudicating, and differentiate tf_001 from the staring/cry/villain 'break-your-face' cluster. tf_004 stays a keeper but should be made head-to-head. Fix the four flagged cards, add the belt meta, grow the roster to ~24 in the tf_006/009/010 register, and this goes from a fun-but-repetitive interlude to a spike people actively want to trigger.

## THE FIX LIST (implement ALL of these)
[1] TARGET: DECK
    PROBLEM: 10 fixed duels is fatal for a palate-cleanser that fires 2-3x/night — one session burns a third of the pool and Night 2 is nearly all reruns. Two lenses call variety a hard NO. Effective variety is even thinner (~5-6) because 3 duds get skipped and the 3 best get over-picked and burn out.
    REQUIRED ACTION: Expand the pool to ~24 duels. This is the single highest-leverage change; hold every new one to the tf_006/009/010 register (a performance aimed AT the table, never a kid's-birthday game).

[2] TARGET: DECK
    PROBLEM: Two framings of the same core defect flagged by all three: the mechanic sells a PHYSICAL spike but 8-9 of 10 are talk/perform, and those 8 collapse into one identical loop — perform a 10s bit, the room votes who committed harder. By the 4th performance duel the crowd-roar is muscle memory and nobody's body left their seat.
    REQUIRED ACTION: Cap crowd-judged performance at ~half the deck; put the variety in HOW you win. Add genuinely physical, visible, objective duels: wall-sit-off, balance-on-one-foot-while-insulting, last-to-flinch, dance-battle-no-music, mirror-copy-off, forced-escalation ('whoever quits loses').

[3] TARGET: DECK
    PROBLEM: The mechanic literally hands out 'the belt' every fight and then throws it away — every duel is a disconnected one-off, so nothing compounds across the night. A duel game's real one-more-round engine is a running rivalry, and it's absent.
    REQUIRED ACTION: Add a persistent champion meta: the belt-holder DEFENDS next TITLEFIGHT, the loser can call one grudge rematch, and forfeits (tf_002's 'silent next round') stack. Converts disposable one-offs into an escalating session-long rivalry.

[4] TARGET: tf_008
    PROBLEM: 'Trash talk about your opponent's TECHNIQUE' references a technique/contest that doesn't exist — the target is undefined, so fighters freeze with nothing concrete to attack. Flagged by two lenses. Cheap, clear fix.
    REQUIRED ACTION: Anchor the bark to a real roastable target: texting style, driving, dating history — 'all bark, zero facts, maximum confidence.' Vague becomes vicious.

[5] TARGET: tf_005
    PROBLEM: Fake-cry-off is funny but broken two ways: 'first believable sob wins' may never resolve (crying on command is hard — two constipated faces, no winner) AND it crowns the boring outcome; the comedy is in how catastrophically BAD people are at it.
    REQUIRED ACTION: Reward the disaster and time-box it: '15s each, ugliest/wettest/most PATHETIC theatrical breakdown wins, first to crack into a laugh forfeits.' Guarantees resolution and aims at the choke-laugh.

[6] TARGET: tf_002
    PROBLEM: Thumb war is the deck's weakest card by consensus — playground filler that's silent, low-visibility, and SELF-adjudicating (whoever pins wins), so it bypasses the crowd-vote entirely and drops energy on a 'spike.' (Note: its clean objective win-condition is the right SHAPE; the execution is the problem.)
    REQUIRED ACTION: Keep the objective format but make the mouths the show: both live-commentate their own hand like a PPV title fight, first to run out of breathless commentary loses — or swap for a visible physical duel. Don't ship the silent version.

[7] TARGET: tf_001
    PROBLEM: Staring contest resolves cleanly but is the most GENERIC card in the deck (drops into any party game unchanged) and clusters with tf_005/tf_006 as a third 'first to break your face loses' shape. Two lenses flag it.
    REQUIRED ACTION: Differentiate: keep eye contact while the crowd actively tries to crack you (heckle/whisper-compliment in a threatening voice) — adds words, menace, and the crowd into a dead silent bit, and de-clusters it from 005/006.

[8] TARGET: tf_007
    PROBLEM: Air-instrument solo is a charades-night staple — about a fake guitar, not about these friends. Fun for 8 seconds, forgotten instantly; reads as the first physical-party-game trope an AI reaches for. Two lenses flag it (one calls it a solid secondary — minority view).
    REQUIRED ACTION: Point the performance AT the table: 'most insufferable air-set imaginable while screaming your opponent's name like your worst ex' — or swap for an impersonation-strut. Mine the friend group, not a Stratocaster.

[9] TARGET: tf_004
    PROBLEM: Keeper, but not truly head-to-head: each fighter reenacts THEIR OWN embarrassing fall, so it's two unrelated solo bits of different personal content judged on vague 'commitment,' and it dies if a player has no memorable fall. (Two lenses love it; fit judge caught the structural flaw — the flaw is real and cheap to fix.)
    REQUIRED ACTION: Make it comparable: both reenact the SAME prompt ('slow-mo walk into a glass door'), or flip to reenacting the OTHER fighter's most embarrassing moment — now it's head-to-head, roast-native, and needs no one's own material.

## CROSS-GAME DIRECTIVES (apply to this deck too)
- FIX THE ARC GRAMMAR SO SPOTLIGHT GAMES REACH DEPTH 5 AND 7 (packages/engine/src/arc.ts). Verified: at depth 5 the forced placements — simultaneous opener at slot 0, scatter at mid(2), bargain at bargainSlotFor(5)=3, all-players-score finale at 4 — consume every slot at idx>=3, and the spotlight gate blocks idx<3. Depth 7 is identical: scatter at 3, titlefight at 5, bargain at 4, finale at 6. Result: CONFESSION, POISON, REDFLAG and ALIBI can only ever be dealt in a Full Damnation.
  WHY: This is the single highest-leverage item in the entire review and no deck-level chair could see it. Confession is the best-scoring deck in the box (8/8/7) and the default 'Standard Descent' never deals it. Most groups' first and possibly only night is drawn from just four decks with sanctioned repeats. Every card fix below is worth less than making the good decks actually reachable. Loosen the spotlight gate to idx>=2, or make the bargain/finale slots spotlight-eligible, or drop the forced titlefight at depth 7 — then re-run the 1000-seed property tests.
- BREAK THE CROSS-DECK ROMANCE MONOCULTURE AT E4/E5. Cap romance/exes/hookups at roughly one third of every deck's deep tiers and backfill with non-romance archetypes (the cheap one, the petty accountant, the snitch, the grudge-holder, the menace, table-aware implication). Roast E4/E5 is almost all dating; confession's entire E5 is sexual; overunder's finale is ~15 flavors of ex-pining; fillin's E5 is sex/shame confessions; redflag's summit is partner-scorecards.
  WHY: Five of nine chairs found this independently INSIDE their own deck without knowing it was universal. Across a full night it compounds catastrophically: every game's biggest moment lands on the same topic, so the same one or two 'romantic' people get named at every climax. That is typecasting, it is the fastest way to make one specific friend stop wanting to play, and the consent ceilings do not catch it because it is a frequency problem, not an exposure-tier problem.
- REBUILD THE MIDDLES (E2/E3), NOT THE PEAKS — this is the 'everyone has fun' fix. Every deck's identified sag block sits in its mid tier: roast 082-096, poison 048-067, scatter's 34-card E3 plateau, overunder's E2 (half the deck), alibi's funeral belly, fillin's court-record family, redflag's roommate band. Thread table-aware 'someone in this room' spikes through these middles rather than banking them at the end.
  WHY: consent.ts caps a mixed room at the second-lowest ceiling (min-of-all at N<=4), which means any group with one cautious friend plays almost entirely in E2/E3 — exactly the material nine councils just condemned. Right now the game's quality is inversely correlated with how mixed the group is. Fixing the middles also fixes 'the night sags before it peaks' for every group, so this single pass serves both questions the owner asked.
- GIVE EVERY DECK A REAL C2/E1 ON-RAMP. Roast's chaos floor never drops below C3, confession's floor is C3, redflag OPENS at full menace (go-bag/vanish, padlocked chest freezer, deadbolt on the outside sitting in E1). Add a small low-stakes-but-still-pointed opening rung to each deck and demote the misplaced menace cards out of E1.
  WHY: Three chairs flagged this separately. The first two circles are when a mixed group decides whether this is their kind of game or something they need to survive. Opening hot is how you lose the two most cautious people at the table in the first ten minutes — and once they disengage, the rest of the night's consensus votes have fewer live candidates and the whole engine degrades.
- GLOBAL TEMPLATE-FATIGUE CULL: cut roughly 10-15% of every deck, specifically its dominant repeated skeleton. Fillin's ~20 'official record euphemizes my crime' cards, redflag's ~25-30 scorecard cards, poison's ~20 annoying-friend block, alibi's four rigid category-triplet templates (each running 15-26 cards straight), scatter's ~50-of-81 first-person confessional shape, overunder's ~25 physical stunts, confession's ~15 self-justification tags. Target ~120-150 cards deleted corpus-wide.
  WHY: 987 cards is the headline number; effective variety is far lower and every chair said so in different words. Template repetition is what makes a table say 'this again' on night two, and it is the specific tell the project's own taste law bans as content-farm output. Elimination before addition is already the house rule — the corpus is big enough to cut hard without falling below coverage.
