POISON PITCH: two players are dealt OPPOSITE sides of a cursed either/or (optionA vs optionB), each SELLS their side out loud, the room votes on who pitched the meaner/funnier one.

A card PASSES when: both options are genuinely cursed, genuinely arguable, and fun to defend.

A card FAILS when any of these are true:
- **Swap test fail:** the card works unchanged in a generic party game (Cards Against Humanity / Truth-or-Dare / "which friend are you"). That's GENERIC.
- **No dilemma:** one option is the obvious pick, OR both options are the SAME vice (no tension to argue). e.g. "announce your salary || announce your outfit cost" — both are just "money oversharer."
- **Not cursed:** one side is actually a blessing/whimsy, so nobody would defend the other. e.g. a "silent parade of animals follows you" is nice, not a curse.
- **Toothless:** neither side is mean or cursed, just a neutral quirk (boring vs weird food order).
- **Cruel not funny:** tips from affectionate roast into genuine wound with no comedy — pity disclosure, "you never asked how I'm doing." Different failure from generic; flag separately.

**How to apply:** When auditing this deck, score each card GENERIC/SAFE-GOOD/CONTEXTUAL/DISTINCTIVE and separately flag cruel/wound cards. See [[poison-systemic-taste-debt]] for the recurring failure clusters.
