RED FLAG RALLY: each card is a dating FIGHT CARD — one PERK worth wanting + one RED FLAG worth running from, about one fictional date. A defender talks the room into SMASHING despite the flag; the room votes SMASH/PASS.

A card PASSES when: the perk is genuinely appealing, the flag is genuinely alarming AND specific AND weird, and there's a big funny GAP between them that's fun to defend.

A card FAILS when any of these are true:
- **Swap test fail:** the flag works verbatim in any dating game or a Cosmo "red flags" listicle. The platonic-generic red flags to watch for: "ex still has a key," "saves an ex's number," "keeps score in arguments," "still hung up on their ex." These are GENERIC.
- **Lazy flag:** "is a criminal / murderer / cheater" with no specific weird twist. SAFE-GOOD at best.
- **Weak perk collapses the gap:** if the perk isn't worth wanting (e.g. "knows when to grab a jacket"), there's no dilemma to defend, so the card dies regardless of the flag.
- **Same-energy:** perk and flag are the same register with no tension. Broken card.
- **Redundancy:** the premise repeats another card's template (see [[descent-content-systemic-debt]]). Generic-by-repetition even when the copy is good.

House tone reference: low-alarm-but-weird flags (wetsuit at the door, forty named spiders) are fine and on-voice — the dilemma there is "adorable-weird vs deal-breaker-weird." What is NOT on-voice: real-world coercive-control flags played straight with no comedic euphemism, and any flag that reads as an abuse-awareness PSA rather than dive-bar-2am dark comedy.

The strongest cards tie the flag to the PERK's profession and land a coined phrase: watchmaker who sets clocks eleven minutes fast ("won't say what happened at eleven"), pilot who calls the relationship "the pilot" that won't get renewed, masseur who "never rushes you" but starts a stopwatch when you cry.

**How to apply:** score each card GENERIC/SAFE-GOOD/CONTEXTUAL/DISTINCTIVE on individual merit, THEN apply the redundancy law across the batch, and separately flag any card that tips from affectionate-violence into genuine cruelty. See [[poison-card-quality-bar]] for the sibling POISON deck.
