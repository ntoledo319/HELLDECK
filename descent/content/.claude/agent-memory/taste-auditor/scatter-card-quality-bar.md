SCATTERBLAST: every phone shows a CATEGORY + a single LETTER. The room shouts answers that fit the category AND start with that letter while an invisible bomb with a hidden fuse passes around; whoever is blanking when it blows loses.

A card PASSES when BOTH are true:
- **Taste:** the category has a POV and survives the swap test. A GENERIC scatter card is "Animals / Countries / Fruits" — anything from a children's word game. The deck's never-word is WHOLESOME, so tame relatable icebreakers ("what's in your junk drawer", "things you keep meaning to fix", "habits your dentist nags about") FAIL even though adults can play them — they'd headline a family party game unchanged.
- **Playability:** the (letter, category) pairing yields ~4+ plausible real answers. A funny category on a dead letter is a broken card. Always brainstorm the actual answer set on the given letter before passing — the failure is usually a letter that clusters (e.g. "things this group hides from parents" on W = weed/wine/whiskey, all booze) or forces answers ("reasons someone got rejected" on R = reeked/rambled/rude).

Where the strength lives (confirmed in the 059–094 batch): the E1 absurdist/menace categories ("cargo on a suspicious submarine", "cursed items at the estate sale") and the E4/E5 table-aware "someone here…" roast categories ("shows someone here fakes having seen", "conspiracies someone here fully believes", "charges someone here has beaten") are the DISTINCTIVE core — the "someone here" framing does the specificity work by aiming at the room.

**Dedup clusters to watch (generic-by-repetition even when copy is fine):**
- snacking: fridge-snacks / over-the-sink-snacks / existing off-the-floor-snacks — keep ONE.
- excuses: running-late / vanishing / existing boss+fire-dept+bouncer+recital pile — crowded.
- single vs rejected — near-duplicate roast premise (and both flirt with a real romantic-undesirability wound, not a behavior roast — the closest this deck gets to cruel).
- drunk: "did drunk and denied" vs "blames on tequila" — the tequila one is sharper; the denied one is the vague dup.
- stolen-sober vs existing crimes-committed-sober (both letter S).

**Cross-deck escalation:** the relatable/petty-domestic MIDDLE tier (SCATTER's E2 C3) regresses to wholesome-generic — the exact same failure mode as the POISON deck's E3 "annoying-friend-habit" middle tier. Two decks now confirm it: the tame relatable-domestic band is where a hand-written batch quietly reverts to the mean, while the absurdist top and the table-aware roast tier stay sharp. Treat "audit the mild relatable middle hardest" as a standing rule for any HELLDECK deck. See [[poison-systemic-taste-debt]] and [[poison-card-quality-bar]].
