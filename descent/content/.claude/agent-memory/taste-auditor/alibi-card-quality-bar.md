ALIBI DROP: the ACCUSED is handed an ACCUSATION (a deranged scenario to explain away) + 3 contraband words to secretly smuggle into a 30-second spoken lie; the jury then guesses which 3 of 8 lineup words were forced. The audit is about the ACCUSATION LINE only — the plant/decoy words are machine-verified unique, don't re-check them.

Cards are tagged `[E# C# register]` — E = Exposure tier, C = Chaos. Three tiers behave differently:
- **E3 = absurdist surreal crimes** (funeral/food/gross-object Monty-Python register). Not table-aware. Strong when the image is unforgettable or carries a voice-quote euphemism ("to hear the ocean better," checked the meat slicer in "as your plus one").
- **E4 = table-aware friend-group indictments** ("someone in this room…"). This is the HELLDECK sweet spot — these encode the friend-roast identity, so they clear the DISTINCTIVE bar most easily.
- **E5 = aimed at the room**, highest exposure.

A great accusation = a deranged, SPECIFIC, funny scenario with a POV that begs to be lied about. A GENERIC one = bland "explain why you were late," no fingerprint.

PASS bar:
- **Swap test:** works unchanged in a generic party game? GENERIC, cut. (E3 caveat: a merely dark-absurdist image could live in ANY edgy game — that's CONTEXTUAL at best, not DISTINCTIVE. DISTINCTIVE in E3 requires a singular voice; in E4/E5 it comes free from friend-group specificity.)
- **Concrete image:** the scenario must paint one crisp deranged picture. Vague intrigue ("you knew the surprise party was a decoy") fails as SAFE-GOOD — competent menace framing with no picture.
- **Cruelty is STRUCTURALLY LOW here vs POISON:** accusations are fictional crimes the accused performs innocence of — nobody believes them, so no real trait is exposed. This insulates ALIBI from the genuine-wound risk that plagues POISON confessions. Only flag a card if it's framed as plausible-true AND targets a specific vulnerable member (e.g. ranking friends by "least likely to notice they're gone").
