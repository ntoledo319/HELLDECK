The line **"Whatever your face is doing — stop it."** is a deliberate HELLDECK
waiting-screen closer for the "you're being judged, you can't act now" beat. It
appears with a game-specific lead-in on three screens:

- `screens/confession.tsx` (Jury waiting) — "They're deciding your fate. …"
- `screens/poison.tsx` (Vote waiting) — "They're deciding which of you sold it harder. …"
- `screens/alibi.tsx` (Hunt waiting) — "They're picking your story apart word by word. …"

**Why:** The refrain works as a brand motif because each instance leads with a
distinct game-specific taunt; the shared closer is intentional, not a template
fill. Confession is the established original.

**How to apply:** Treat 3 as the ceiling. A 4th verbatim reuse tips it from
"signature refrain" into wallpaper (fails the Swap Test — the closer alone would
sit on any waiting screen unchanged). If a new game needs a waiting-screen closer,
write a fresh one rather than reaching for this. Flag any 4th instance on review.
